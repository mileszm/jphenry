Joseph P. Henry

Hi, I’m a writer and Ph.D. Candidate in the art history program at the CUNY Graduate Center. My research interests have centered on German modernism; the relationship between art and labor; the history of modern painting; primitivism and race; performance; and queer visual culture. I’ve been focusing on three broader projects over the last couple of years. The first, my dissertation, argues for the structuring prominence of the machine and industrial capitalism for German Expressionism, in particular the art of Die Brücke. The second considers the estranged “thing” as a mediation of political crisis in realist modes of interwar German art. The third analyzes the appropriation of Western modernism by contemporary queer painters.

I’ve participated in the Whitney Independent Study Program, acted as Scholar-in-Residence at LACMA’s Robert Gore Rifkind Center for German Expressionist Studies, worked at the Dia Art Foundation, and taught at Brooklyn College. I’m currently living in Berlin, working on my dissertation with the support of the Deutscher Akademischer Austausch Dienst (DAAD) and sponsored by the Brücke Museum. You can find my writing, talks, and course syllabi at the menu on the left.
