Joseph Henry

I’m a writer and Ph.D. Candidate in the art history program at the CUNY Graduate Center. My research interests have centered on German modernism; the relationship between art and labor; the history of modern painting; primitivism and race; performance; and queer visual culture. I’ve been focusing on three broader projects over the last couple of years. The first, my dissertation, argues for the structuring prominence of the machine and industrial production for German Expressionism, in particular the art of Die Brücke. The second considers the estranged “thing” as a mediation of political crisis in realist modes of interwar German art. The third analyzes the appropriation of Western modernism by contemporary queer painters.

I’ve participated in the Whitney Independent Study Program, acted as Scholar-in-Residence at LACMA’s Robert Gore Rifkind Center for German Expressionist Studies, worked at the Dia Art Foundation and Artists Space, and taught at Brooklyn College. Last year, I lived in Berlin, working on my dissertation with the support of the Deutscher Akademischer Austausch Dienst (DAAD) and sponsored by the Brücke Museum. This year, I’m a Mellon-Marron Museum Research Consortium Fellow in the Department of Drawings and Prints at the Museum of Modern Art. You can find my writing, talks, and course syllabi at the menu on the left.

Email me at jhenry@gradcenter.cuny.edu. I’d be happy to share any texts or lecture slides not available here.
